{{count()}} English or German?

Your choice! You may (a) write your thesis in English or German, (b) hold your presentation in English or German, (c) prepare your slides in English or German.
While you may combine these three options (e.g. write in English, slides in English, talk in German), don't mix up languages inside a task (e.g. no English-German mix in thesis - except for abstract).
I recommend English - at least for you written thesis!

{{count()}} Thesis defense: how to present

Questions: - Contribution? - Methodology? - How do you know that ...? -> Reference related work - Why didn't you take [related work] into account? - Why did you choose the definition by ... ? - How would you define ... ?

{{count()}} How to write: structure

Writing your thesis is similar to writing a research paper. Focus on a clear, logical story line.
Tell your story to a friend: what are you doing? What's the problem? How do you solve it?
Draft your structure rather early in the process, and fill it with content remarks ('What will I write here?').
Apart from classic elements like abstract, introduction, related work or conclusion, structural elements depend of course on topic, work and your individual style.

Abstract

→ Write your abstract at the end. It should be a short, appealing summary of your work.

How to quality-check your abstract (method by Prof. Albrecht Schmidt):

Write your abstract and try to allocate each sentence to the questions below (e.g. by marking text with different colors).
→ Whatâ€™s the large scope of the problem space? Why should anybody care?
→ What is the specific problem addressed?
→ Why is the problem important? Why did we work on this?
→ What have we done?
→ What is new about the work?
→ What did we find out? What are concrete results?
→ What are the implications on a larger scale? How does the paper change the world?
Rewrite if questions (colors) are mixed up or missing. Remove irrelevant sentences.

Introduction

→ Describe the motivation (problem, examples) for your work.
→ Sketch your solution, your research questions and the results.
→ Give an overview of the structure of your thesis.

Related Work

→ What did others do in relevant areas (state of the art)?
→ What problems are there?
→ What do other approaches lack? What will we do about it?
